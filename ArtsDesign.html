Arts and Design

About

Visual arts brings together several different art forms, including both fine arts, such as drawing, painting, photography, and sculpture, as well as handicrafts, such as basketry, ceramics, textiles, and jewelry. Many undergraduate visual arts degrees offer a basic overview of studio arts and give students opportunities to explore a variety of art forms. Many artistic disciplines such as performing arts, conceptual art, and textile arts also involve aspects of visual arts as well as arts of other types.

Graduates from courses of the Arts and Design study option will be critically reflective design practitioners with a broad variety of speciality abilities in a major of their choosing. They will be familiar with design processes and theories, as well as possess specialised technical knowledge and skills. They will be able to think creatively, solve problems in a flexible manner, and express design concepts fluently, intelligently, and with style.

Careers

Your specialisation in Arts and Design alongside with your personal interests and chosen electives can impact and help you decide the job you want to pursue. With all these skills Art and Design courses teach you, it will be easy for you to succeed in any industry, even internationally.
