University of Otago

New Zealand's first university, Otago, was established in 1869. Otago has prided itself on being a research-driven university with an international reputation for excellence.

Find out about the University of Otago

The University of Otago covers from Auckland in the north to Invercargill in the south. While the majority of our students study at the main, beautiful Dunedin campus, many of our postgraduate students live on Health Science-focused campuses in Christchurch and Wellington.

Employers value well-rounded, practical, adaptive, and resilient graduates, and the university has a reputation for creating them. Students at Otago study more than simply academics; they also gain life skills, creative thinking, problem-solving, and team dynamics. During their time here, students are encouraged to participate in a variety of sporting, cultural, and community-based activities in addition to their studies.

The Campus

From the historic Clocktower Building to world-class teaching and research labs, performance spaces, lecture theatres, sporting facilities, and study areas, including our award-winning Central Library, Otago is an exciting mix of old and new. Otago University provides students with a unique campus experience as well as a vibrant, sociable student culture. Dunedin is home to the majority of Otago's 21,000 students.
